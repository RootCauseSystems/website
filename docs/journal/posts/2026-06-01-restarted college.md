# Back to the Baseline: Upgrading the Architecture 

The Log:

In security and compliance, you can't protect a system you don't fully understand. The same logic applies to career infrastructure. After successfully establishing my foundation with an Associate of Applied Science, it is time to expand the perimeter. Today marks my official return to Western Governors University (WGU) to complete my Bachelor of Science in Cybersecurity and Information Assurance.

The Objective: 
Transform operational experience into certified, academic frameworks that map directly to industry standards.

The Logic of the Pivot:
Operating high-consequence systems—whether in a nuclear submarine or a hardened Linux environment—requires continuous verification. Earning this degree isn't just about checking a box; it is about formalizing my practical knowledge of network defense, risk management, and secure systems architecture. The WGU competency-based model fits perfectly with my operational mindset: prove you know the system, pass the assessment, and move forward. 

The Resource Allocation (Rudisill Impact):
This academic upgrade was made possible through a critical change in how the Department of Veterans Affairs allocates education benefits. Thanks to the *Rudisill v. McDonough* Supreme Court decision on April 16, 2024, I was awarded an additional 12 months of GI Bill entitlement. 

Previously, veterans eligible for both the Montgomery GI Bill (MGIB) and the Post-9/11 GI Bill (PGIB) were required to waive their MGIB eligibility, effectively capping their total benefits at 36 months. The Supreme Court ruled that veterans with at least two separate periods of qualifying service may access both programs for a maximum combined total of 48 months. With my 20-year Navy career providing multiple qualifying periods, this restored a full year of funding to cover the WGU tuition.

### How to Get Your GI Bill Evaluated Under Rudisill

If you served multiple enlistments and previously exhausted or waived your GI Bill benefits, you may be entitled to an extension. Here is the current procedure based on updated VA directives:

1. **The Automated Baseline:** You likely do not need to take manual action. The VA has updated its systems to automatically evaluate the files of all veterans with possible eligibility. They are no longer requiring veterans to submit a request for a review to learn about their earned benefits. Once the file is evaluated, you will receive an official decision via U.S. mail. 
2. **The Active Application:** If you are actively enrolling in school and want to trigger a change of program, you can submit a Request for Change of Program or Place of Training via VA Form 22-1995. The online version of VA Form 22-1995 explicitly prompts you with a question asking if your request is related to the Rudisill decision. Be sure to select this option on page two of the online application.
3. **The Expiration Recalculation:** If your benefits previously expired because you forfeited your remaining MGIB benefits to use the PGIB, the VA will calculate a new expiration date. The strict deadline to submit a claim to receive this delimiting (expiration) date recalculation is October 1, 2030.
